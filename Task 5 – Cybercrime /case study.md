                                                 Case Study: Ransomware Attack
                                                 
Ransomware is a type of cybercrime in which malicious software encrypts a victim’s data and demands payment (usually in cryptocurrency) to restore access. A well-
known real-life example is the WannaCry ransomware attack in 2017, which affected hundreds of thousands of computers worldwide, including hospitals, businesses, and government organizations.
The attack typically begins when a user unknowingly downloads a malicious file or clicks on a harmful link. In the WannaCry case, the ransomware spread through a vulnerability in outdated Windows systems.
Once inside, it quickly encrypted files on the system and displayed a message demanding payment in Bitcoin to unlock them. It also spread automatically to other computers on the same network.
The primary targets were organizations with weak cybersecurity measures, especially those using outdated software. Hospitals in the UK’s NHS were heavily affected, 
leading to canceled appointments and disrupted services.
The consequences of ransomware attacks include loss of important data, financial damage, and operational disruption. In critical sectors like healthcare, it can even impact human safety.
This incident highlights the importance of regular software updates, data backups, and cybersecurity awareness to prevent such attacks.
